IL6S Methodology
Manufacturing
▼
Integrated Lean 6 Sigma (IL6S) is Mondelēz's proprietary CI framework combining Lean Manufacturing and Six Sigma into 10 integrated pillars — Safety, Quality, Cost, Delivery, Morale, and more.

In practice, I led loss tree analysis to identify the biggest OEE gaps, facilitated Focused Improvement (FI) kaizen events targeting chronic losses, and maintained CI pillar boards with weekly cadence. Key metric: OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality.
  • Availability loss: unplanned downtime, changeover
  • Performance loss: minor stops, speed losses
  • Quality loss: defects, rework
📉
Depreciation Calculation
Finance / Engineering
▼
In manufacturing and restaurant contexts, correctly calculating asset depreciation is critical for true cost of production.

Straight-line: (Cost − Salvage) ÷ Useful Life. Simple, predictable. Used for stable equipment.
Declining Balance: Book Value × Rate. Front-loads expense; better for tech assets that lose value fast.

In my cost-analysis dashboard for Re:booze, I incorporate equipment depreciation per unit into the BOM cost so that menu prices truly reflect long-term capex — not just ingredient cost.
📊
Contribution Margin & OR
Operations Research
▼
Contribution Margin (CM) = Revenue − Variable Costs. It tells you how much each unit sold contributes toward fixed costs and profit. CM ratio = CM ÷ Revenue.

Operational Research (OR) techniques like linear programming can optimise the product mix to maximise total CM subject to constraints (machine hours, raw material limits).

I apply this in menu engineering: identifying which menu items have high CM but low popularity ('Puzzles') vs. high CM and high popularity ('Stars') and using data to drive promotional decisions.
📡
MQTT IoT Protocol
Software / IoT
▼
MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport) is a lightweight publish-subscribe protocol ideal for constrained IoT devices. Devices publish to topics; subscribers receive messages instantly.

Architecture I use:
  • ESP32 (sensor node) publishes temperature/order data to broker
  • MQTT Broker (e.g. Mosquitto / HiveMQ) routes messages
  • Node.js server subscribes and writes to Supabase
  • Dashboard receives real-time updates via Supabase Realtime
QoS levels: 0 (fire-and-forget), 1 (at least once), 2 (exactly once).
🧫
Fermentation Science
Brewing / Biochemistry
▼
Brewing is applied biochemistry. Yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae) converts fermentable sugars into ethanol and CO₂ via glycolysis, while producing flavour compounds (esters, fusel alcohols, diacetyl).

Key variables I control:
  • Pitch rate: underpitching → stressed yeast → fusel alcohol
  • Temperature: affects ester/fusel balance; lager strains need 8–12 °C
  • Gravity: OG vs FG determines ABV = (OG − FG) × 131.25
  • Attenuation: how efficiently yeast consumed sugars
My fermentation monitoring system tracks these in real-time.
🔗
BOM & Cost Engineering
Manufacturing / F&B
▼
A Bill of Materials (BOM) is the complete list of ingredients, sub-assemblies, and quantities needed to produce one unit of a product. In F&B, it's the recipe cost breakdown.

My cost-analysis system calculates:
  • Standard cost per dish = Σ (ingredient qty × unit price)
  • Actual vs standard variance to flag wastage
  • Food cost % = Ingredient cost ÷ Selling price
  • Break-even units = Fixed costs ÷ CM per unit
This gives management a live view of whether each menu item is profitable at current ingredient prices.
